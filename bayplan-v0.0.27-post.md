The tool has been telling you when something is wrong for a long time now. A clearance halo turning red. A machine hanging past a wall. A circuit over its breaker, a dust branch coming up short, a compressor that cannot keep up, a light with nothing to plug into. All good warnings. The trouble is they were scattered, a red edge here, a note in the utility panel there, and nothing that let you stand back and ask the only question that matters before you start moving heavy things: is this plan actually ready?

Now there is one list, and one button, and it answers exactly that.

## The badge

Up in the header there is a Ready button, and it wears a little badge that never lies to you. When the plan is clean it is a green check. When it is not, it is a count, in red if something is truly wrong, amber if it is just a caution. So you do not have to go looking for trouble. You are laying out the shop, you glance up, and the badge tells you where you stand. Fix a conflict and watch the number tick down. That is the whole loop, and it is a good one.

## The list

Click it and you get everything, in one place, sorted so the real problems are at the top. The clearance conflicts, the machines past the walls, the door that swings into the jointer. The loads with no panel, the circuit carrying more than its breaker wants, the outlet asking for 240 where only 120 is run. The dust branch that will not pull enough air, the compressor that cannot feed every tool at once. The panel out of spaces. The service that cannot carry the demand, with the size it would take. All of it, grouped by system, errors first, so you work down the list and clear it.

And when there is nothing left to clear, it tells you so, plainly: a green check and the word that every one of these features was quietly building toward. Ready to build. It prints, too, as a check-off sheet with a box in front of every item, so you can walk the shop and tick them off, or hand it to whoever is helping and say clear these.

## Why it was nearly free

Here is the thing I like about this one. It is not a new check. It does not know anything the tool did not already know. Every item on it comes from a check that was already running, TRIM for the clearances, the utility engine for the wiring and the dust and the air, the panel schedule and the service calc for capacity. The punch list just gathers them, sorts them, and puts a box in front of each one. Which means it does not go stale: as those checks get smarter, the list gets smarter with them, for nothing. That is the payoff of having built each check as a real thing that returns real findings, instead of just painting something red on the screen. When you finally want the whole picture, the pieces are all there to collect.

Five new checks, on the aggregator itself: that an empty plan reads clear, that unfed loads show up as errors, that a service overload lands in the right group, and that errors sort above warnings. A hundred and sixty-two checks now, green, which feels right for the release that is, itself, about watching the count go green.

One button. One list. One question, answered. Is it ready? Now you can just look.

Make. Hack. Learn. Share. Repeat.
